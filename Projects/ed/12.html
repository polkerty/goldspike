Math Tracks

As a highschool student, you have four years to gain solid skills in Algebra, Geometry, and Trigonometry. These courses lay important groundwork for college courses like Calculus, which is a prerequisite for training for nursing, engineering, computer science or IT, and many other jobs. If you don't take it by your first year of college, you will severely limit your job choices and your potential future earnings. Some of the following math tracks allow you to take Calculus in highschool, and others prepare you to take it right out of highschool. We will also discuss the main math courses you'll see in college if you major in engineering or another course with a lot of math.

First of all, we have one important piece of advice: Start planning your math track as early as you can to leave yourself a slack year. You may have to retake a course, or you may find in a few years that you want to do more math A good target year is Grade 7.

Grade 7 is your first year in Junior High.
