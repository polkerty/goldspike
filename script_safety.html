JavaScript: Should You Turn it On?

The Benefits & Drawbacks

 Forrest Brazeal (Last Modified October 20, 2011)

Javascript is on in your browser.

JavaScript is the code that websites use to be interactive and functional. This website, for instance, uses JavaScript to run its quiz page, audio clip page, news pages, and interactive widgets. To really use this website, you need JavaScript enabled. Most websites rely heavily on JavaScript: Google, Ebay, CNN, and so on.

What can JavaScript do?

JavaScript is used to make the pages on websites dynamic. It can load additional content onto webpages without reloading the page, enabling many, many useful web services.

JavaScript can also be used for the following undesirable purposes:

Danger Can it Be Fixed? Can it Be Ignored?
Pop-up Windows sometimes open behind the current page. You really can't control their content, because they are often ads and thus not created by the website owner.
Note: We do not, have not, and never will open pop-up windows. In fact, we don't carry ads of any kind. 		Fortunately most browsers simply allow you to turn on a "pop-up blocker" which prevents new windows from opening unless you specifically give permission. ( Get a new browser - very important.) Most people choose to turn on both JavaScript and their pop-up blocker. With a good pop-up blocker, the danger/annoyance factor is minimal, though we will note that Internet Explorer's pop-up blocker is somewhat of a nuisance itself. Again, you should get a new browser. The security on such browsers is much greater than on older versions (say, Internet Explorer 7 or older, Firefox 3 or older, Safari 3 or older.)
Scripts that loop forever sometimes freeze the page up or cause other problems. Depending on the browser and computer, you might have to restart the computer and could lose your work. Newer browsers handle such scripts very well, usually giving you a chance to "cancel" them. Such scripts are rare, but you will probably run up against one sometime. Also depending on what you do, you might have to restart your computer on such an occasion.
Downloads onto your computer can contain malware or spyware. No, not really. However, your computer will ask you before it runs any software it downloads. It is to your advantage to pay attention to the warning message. Always ask yourself, before permitting such software to run,
  1. Do I know what this software is for?
  2. Do I know who created it?
  3. Do I need to give it permission to run?

Such bad software is spread through email attachments as well. You may want to do some additional research. Also, check to see if you have an anti-virus program installed. 		Scripts really aren't the reason malware gets onto your computer. Malware files can reach your computer in lots of ways. In the end, though, you must click the button permitting the program to install. With good anti-virus software, you aren't at much more of a risk with scripts on than off.
Web beacons and cookies can invade your privacy by keeping track of your web history and other statistics.
Note: We have a very strict privacy policy and do not collect any of your information beyond what you specifically give us. All transactions are carried out through a secure third party vendor. Our privacy policy. 		Certain precautions greatly reduce what personally identifiable information (PII) can be tracked. Start by setting your browser to reject all cookies except from the current website. Also, check to see if the page you are entering your information in is a HTTPS:// address. If there are any red or warning sorts of icons in the browser bar, don't enter your information. In general, be cautious about where you enter your information. If a page asks for a SSN, for example, be very alert. Yes, you can.

The benefits of JavaScript, on the other hand, are very high. JavaScript allows you to:

  • Log in to websites.
  • Use online services such as Google Maps.
  • Use almost any website to a much fuller extent. Websites are generally enhanced in functionality and appearance when JavaScript is enabled.

So, in general, we would encourage you to turn JavaScript ON, at least for this website. You will greatly improve your experience if you do so.
